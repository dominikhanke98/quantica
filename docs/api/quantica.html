quantica

quantica — a tested and validated quantitative-finance library.

Phase 1 exposes the pricing primitives: the option contract (~quantica.pricing.instruments.EuropeanOption), the market dynamics (~quantica.pricing.processes.BlackScholesProcess), and the shared enums (~quantica.core.types.OptionType). Numerical engines are added in later steps and attached to an instrument via set_engine.

class AnalyticEuropeanEngine:

Black--Scholes--Merton closed-form pricer for a EuropeanOption.

Satisfies the ~quantica.pricing.engines.GreeksEngine protocol: provides both calculate (price) and greeks (analytic delta, gamma, vega, theta, rho). The engine is stateless, so one instance can price any number of options and processes.

def calculate( self, instrument: VanillaOption, process: BlackScholesProcess) -> float:

Present value \( V = \omega [S e^{-qT} N(\omega d_1) - K e^{-rT} N(\omega d_2)] \).

In the degenerate \( \sigma\sqrt{T} \to 0 \) limit (zero vol or zero time to expiry) the price collapses to the discounted intrinsic value of the forward, \( \max(\omega (S e^{-qT} - K e^{-rT}), 0) \).

Raises
  • ValueError: If the option is not European (there is no closed form).
def greeks( self, instrument: VanillaOption, process: BlackScholesProcess) -> Greeks:

Analytic first-order Greeks (see ~quantica.pricing.greeks.Greeks).

Raises
  • ValueError: If the option is not European, or \( \sigma = 0 \) or \( T = 0 \), where the Greeks are not well defined (delta becomes a step, gamma a Dirac spike).

@dataclass(frozen=True)
class BlackScholesProcess:

Geometric Brownian motion under the risk-neutral measure.

The spot follows

$$dS_t = (r - q)\, S_t\, dt + \sigma\, S_t\, dW_t,$$

with constant risk-free rate \( r \), continuous dividend yield \( q \), and volatility \( \sigma \) (Black--Scholes--Merton, 1973).

Parameters
  • spot (float): Current underlying price \( S_0 \). Must be positive.
  • rate (float): Continuously-compounded risk-free rate \( r \). May be negative.
  • div (float, optional): Continuous dividend yield \( q \). May be negative; defaults to 0.
  • vol (float): Volatility \( \sigma \), annualised. Must be non-negative; vol == 0 is the deterministic (zero-diffusion) limit.
Notes

Kept as a flat record (spot, rate, vol, div) for the constant-vol case; market exposes the shared Market carrier and from_market() builds one from a market plus a vol. Frozen (immutable).

class FiniteDifferenceEngine:

Crank--Nicolson PDE pricer + Greeks for a vanilla option (European or American).

Parameters
  • space_steps (int, optional): Number of intervals along the log-price axis (default 200). Rounded down to an even number so that ln(spot) lands exactly on a grid node (no interpolation error at the evaluation point).
  • time_steps (int, optional): Number of time steps from maturity back to today (default 200).
  • num_std (float, optional): Half-width of the log-price domain in standard deviations \( \sigma\sqrt{T} \) (default 6), widened if needed to enclose the strike. Larger values push the Dirichlet boundaries further out.
  • rannacher_steps (int, optional): Number of initial Crank--Nicolson steps (nearest expiry) replaced by backward-Euler half-step pairs to damp the payoff-kink oscillation in gamma (default 2). 0 disables Rannacher (pure Crank--Nicolson). Clamped to time_steps.
Notes

Satisfies ~quantica.pricing.engines.GreeksEngine. A European option is solved by a tridiagonal solve per step and is second-order accurate — halving both step sizes cuts the error by ~4. An American option solves the early-exercise LCP per step by projected SOR (see the module docstring).

def greeks( self, instrument: VanillaOption, process: BlackScholesProcess) -> Greeks:

First-order Greeks of instrument under process from the PDE solve.

Delta and gamma are read off the solved value surface at the spot node (chain-rule central differences on the log-grid); theta is a central difference in the time direction; vega and rho are bump-and-reval on \( \sigma \) and \( r \). Rannacher start-up (on by default) keeps the gamma free of the Crank--Nicolson payoff-kink oscillation.

Parameters
  • instrument (VanillaOption): The contract (European or American vanilla).
  • process (BlackScholesProcess): The market dynamics.
Returns
  • Greeks: Delta, gamma, vega, theta and rho (same conventions as ~quantica.pricing.greeks.Greeks).
Raises
  • ValueError: If \( \sigma = 0 \) or \( T = 0 \), where the Greeks are not well defined (delta becomes a step, gamma a Dirac spike).

class HestonProcess:

Heston (1993) stochastic-variance model under the risk-neutral measure.

$$dS_t &= (r - q)\, S_t\, dt + \sqrt{v_t}\, S_t\, dW_t^{(1)}, \ dv_t &= \kappa (\theta - v_t)\, dt + \xi \sqrt{v_t}\, dW_t^{(2)}, \qquad d\langle W^{(1)}, W^{(2)} \rangle_t = \rho\, dt.$$

The variance is a CIR (square-root) process, so it is mean-reverting and non-negative.

Parameters
  • spot (float): Current underlying price \( S_0 \). Must be positive.
  • rate (float): Continuously-compounded risk-free rate \( r \).
  • v0 (float): Initial variance \( v_0 \) (units of variance, i.e. vol squared). >= 0.
  • kappa (float): Mean-reversion speed \( \kappa \). >= 0.
  • theta (float): Long-run variance \( \theta \). >= 0.
  • xi (float): Volatility of variance \( \xi \) ("vol of vol"). >= 0; xi == 0 is the deterministic-variance (Black--Scholes) limit.
  • rho (float): Correlation \( \rho \) between the spot and variance Brownian motions, in [-1, 1].
  • div (float, optional): Continuous dividend yield \( q \). Defaults to 0.
Notes

The Feller condition \( 2\kappa\theta \ge \xi^2 \) (see feller_satisfied) guarantees the variance never reaches zero; a fit that violates it is still valid but the variance can touch zero.

class OptionType(enum.Enum):

The two vanilla option flavours.

The integer value is the payoff sign applied to spot - strike: +1 for a call, -1 for a put. This makes the payoff and the Black--Scholes closed form expressible without branching, e.g. max(sign * (spot - strike), 0).

Intrinsic payoff at a given underlying price.

Computes \( \max(\omega (S - K), 0) \) where \( \omega \) is +1 for a call and -1 for a put. This is the terminal payoff for a European option and the immediate-exercise value at any time for an American one.

  • RuntimeError: If no engine has been attached, or the attached engine does not support Greeks (i.e. is not a ~quantica.pricing.engines.GreeksEngine).

def geometric_asian_price( spot: float, strike: float, rate: float, div: float, vol: float, expiry: float, n_averaging_dates: int, option_type: OptionType) -> float:

Closed-form price of a discretely-monitored geometric-average Asian option.

With monitoring times \( t_i = i\,T/n \) (i = 1..n), \( \ln G \) is normal with

$$\mu_G = \ln S + (r - q - \tfrac12\sigma^2)\,\bar t, \qquad \sigma_G^2 = \frac{\sigma^2}{n^2} \sum_{i,j} \min(t_i, t_j),$$

where \( \bar t \) is the mean monitoring time, giving the Black--Scholes-like value \( e^{-rT}\,\omega\,[\,\mathbb E[G]\,N(\omega d_1) - K\,N(\omega d_2)\,] \).

def heston_characteristic_function( u: numpy.ndarray[tuple[typing.Any, ...], numpy.dtype[numpy.complex128]], tau: float, *, rate: float, div: float, v0: float, kappa: float, theta: float, xi: float, rho: float, spot: float) -> numpy.ndarray[tuple[typing.Any, ...], numpy.dtype[numpy.complex128]]:

Characteristic function of \( \ln S_\tau \) under Heston (little-trap form).

Returns \( \varphi(u) = \mathbb E[e^{i u \ln S_\tau}] \). In the \( \xi \to 0 \) limit the variance is deterministic and this reduces to the Gaussian (Black--Scholes) CF with integrated variance \( \int_0^\tau v_t\,dt \).

def implied_volatility( price: float, option: EuropeanOption, market: Market, *, tol: float = 1e-10, max_iter: int = 100, initial_guess: float | None = None) -> float:

Return the Black--Scholes implied volatility reproducing price.

Parameters
  • price (float): Observed option price to invert.
  • option (EuropeanOption): The contract (strike, expiry, call/put).
  • market (Market): The market state (spot, rate, dividend). Volatility is precisely the unknown being solved for, so — unlike a pricing call — no vol is passed.
  • tol (float, optional): Convergence tolerance on the absolute price residual.
  • max_iter (int, optional): Maximum iterations for both the Newton phase and the Brent fallback.
  • initial_guess (float, optional): Starting volatility for Newton. Defaults to the Brenner--Subrahmanyam at-the-money approximation \( \sigma_0 \approx \sqrt{2\pi/T}\,V/S \).
Returns
  • float: The implied volatility \( \sigma > 0 \) (or 0.0 when the price sits on the discounted-intrinsic lower bound).
Raises
  • ValueError: If option.expiry <= 0, or price lies outside the no-arbitrage band (below discounted intrinsic, or at/above the upper bound), so that no finite positive implied volatility exists.
